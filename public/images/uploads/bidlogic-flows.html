Correcting AI Decisions
Two different screens, two different levels of correction
Screen
Review Emails
Corrects the AI classification — was this email correctly identified as an RFQ or not?

Mark as RFQ

The AI missed it — this IS a real RFQ
👤
Click "Mark as RFQ"On emails showing as "Non-RFQ"
📄
A brand new RFQ is createdContent and attachments are downloaded fresh
🤖
Full pipeline runs automaticallyLine items extracted, matching starts, etc.
When to use
A customer sent a quote request but BidLogic didn't recognize it as one. You see it in the "Non-RFQ" tab and know it should be processed.

Mark as non-RFQ

The AI was wrong — this is NOT an RFQ
👤
Click "Mark as non-RFQ"On emails showing as "RFQ"
📧
Email reclassified as "Not an RFQ"AI learns from this correction
🚫
Associated RFQ is automatically rejectedRemoved from your dashboard
When to use
The email is spam, an internal message, or a general inquiry — not a real quote request. Also use this if the AI badly misread the email and extracted wrong data — demoting and re-promoting reprocesses everything from scratch.
Pro tip: Reprocessing a badly extracted RFQ
If the AI extracted the wrong line items from an email, you can fix it: Mark as non-RFQ (removes the bad RFQ) → then Mark as RFQ again (creates a fresh RFQ and reprocesses everything from the beginning).
Screen
RFQ Detail Page
Manages the RFQ itself — the email was correctly identified, but you want to control what happens with this specific quote request.

Reject RFQ

Don't want to process this quote request
👤
Click "Reject RFQ" on the detail pageAdd a reason if you want
🚫
RFQ moves to "Rejected" tabHidden from your active queue, processing stops
↩️
Can be restored later if needed
When to use
The email IS a quote request, but you don't want to act on it — duplicate request, customer you don't serve, not worth quoting, or you already handled it outside BidLogic.

Restore RFQ

Undo a rejection — bring it back
👤
Click "Restore" in the Rejected tab
🔄
Status is recalculatedBased on what info was already filled in
✓
RFQ returns to your dashboardShows up in the right tab automatically
When to use
You rejected an RFQ by mistake, or circumstances changed and you now want to process it. All previously filled info is preserved.
